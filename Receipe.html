Delicious Donuts

"A simple, yeast-risen doughnut recipe that delivers light, fluffy rings with a subtle hint of lemon zest. Perfect for frying until golden and dipping in a sweet, classic glaze."

Ingredients

  • Sugar
  • Flour
  • Yeast
  • Lemon
  • Milk

Instructions

  1. Mix the yeast with a warm liquid (like lukewarm milk) and a bit of sugar to let it "bloom" or activate. Combine this mixture with the rest of your flour, sugar, and other wet ingredients (plus the lemon zest/juice for flavor).
  2. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and allow it to rise in a warm place until it has doubled in size. This process is crucial for the light, fluffy texture.
  3. Gently punch down the risen dough to release the gases.Roll the dough out to an even thickness. Use a doughnut cutter or two different-sized round cutters to cut out the doughnut shapes.
  4. Place the cut doughnuts on a baking sheet, cover them loosely, and allow them to rise again until they look noticeably puffy. This second rise, or proofing, ensures they will be light when fried.
  5. Heat oil to the proper temperature (typically around 350 fahreheit or 175 celicus). Carefully fry the doughnuts in batches, flipping them until they are golden brown on both sides.
